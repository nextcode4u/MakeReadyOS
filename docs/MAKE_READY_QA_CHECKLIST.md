# Make Ready QA Checklist

MakeReadyOS includes two checklist templates based on the local paper QA workflow:

- `Make Ready QA - Resident Front Sheet`
- `Make Ready QA - Internal Scope & Follow-Up`

The source PDF used for this workflow lives under `reference/make ready/` and is not a runtime dependency. The raw reference file should stay local and ignored. This document is the curated, public-safe version for operators and contributors.

## Intended Paper Workflow

The checklist is designed for mixed digital and paper operations:

- The front sheet is resident-facing and can be left in the unit after completion.
- The back/internal sheet is for maintenance, office, and management handoff notes.
- The internal sheet can be discarded after completion if the relevant notes/photos/checklists are captured in MakeReadyOS.
- Photos, comments, and checklist completion in the item drawer are the durable record.

## Scope Comes First

Scope should happen before make-ready work starts.

Use blue painter's tape to mark visible concerns inside the unit. Tape is an active in-unit label, not just a reminder.

Recommended tape practice:

- Put tape directly near the issue so it is visible at eye level where practical.
- Write the required action or material on the tape with a marker.
- Include sizes and specific parts when known, such as blind size, `9V battery`, `door stop`, `closet shelf clip`, or `outlet cover`.
- Use tape for paint/sheetrock, cleaning, trash-out, maintenance, missing parts, vendor follow-up, and final QC misses.
- Pull tape only when the marked issue is complete.
- Before marking the unit ready, verify all tape has been pulled.

This keeps the unit readable for techs who do not work primarily from a phone or computer and gives office/management a simple visual system during walks.

## Resident Front Sheet Sections

The front sheet confirms the unit was checked and prepared.

General:

- Pre-walk and documentation completed.
- Left-behind items removed.
- Lights, vents, HVAC, water heater, windows, doors, flooring, walls, shelves, washer/dryer, garage, and general interior condition checked.

Kitchen:

- Stove/oven, dishwasher, microwave or vent hood, refrigerator, sink lines, disposal, hot/cold pressure, cabinets, drawers, and appliance interiors checked.

Bathrooms:

- Drains, leaks, tub/shower, caulk/grout, curtain rod, water pressure, under-sink cabinets, and exhaust fans checked.

Living areas and bedrooms:

- Ceiling fans, window coverings, and screens checked.

Safety and electrical:

- Outlets, switches, polarity/grounding, detectors, breakers, exterior lighting, fire extinguisher if applicable, and pest prevention checked.

Final inspection:

- Unit clean, odor-free, ready, locks/rekey complete, keys/codes/tags ready, mailbox verified, trash cans handled, window stickers/tape removed, and final supervisor walk completed.

Sign-off:

- Unit number.
- Completion date.
- Make-ready tech signature/date.
- Supervisor or manager signature/date.
- Door code and mailbox number if applicable.

## Internal Back Sheet Sections

The internal side is for handoff and follow-up:

- Make Ready
- Cleaning
- Trash Out
- Painting / Sheetrock
- Parts needed
- Rework / Follow-up
- Material needed
- Notes
- Orders or pending upgrades

Use this page for operational notes that should not be resident-facing.

## MakeReadyOS Mapping

In MakeReadyOS:

- Attach the resident-front checklist when a unit is ready for final QA or resident-facing sign-off.
- Attach the internal scope/follow-up checklist during the first walk/scope stage.
- Add photos in the inspection gallery for damage, cleaning, trash-out, vendor proof, and charge candidates.
- Use comments for handoff notes that need to remain after the paper sheet is discarded.
- Use checklist completion as the digital durable record.
